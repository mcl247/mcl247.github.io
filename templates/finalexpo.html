Storytelling and Showcasing Our Work


At the end of every semester, our Marketing and Communications Team put together a final exposition open to students, professors, and anyone interested in our projects. Final exposition is a great opportunity for each team to share their amazing work from the beginning to the end of the semester and discuss future goals and plans. The event includes a short presentation from each project as well as horizontal team within CUSD, and an interactive walk-through exhibition of student work and photo galleries. This is one of the only opportunities all semester to learn about the progress and accomplishments of all our amazing teams. Food and beverages are, of course, always included!

Throughout my semesters on the Marketing and Communications Team, I've realized how important the little things are. Transitioning from a purely presentation-based final exposition my first semester on CUSD to one that is half presentation based and half interactive gallery and walk-through like, I've seen a drastic change in audience involvement. Most people are visual learns, and we strive to provide just that.





Knowing that presentations can be dull and that twelve different teams presenting may become a chaos if there was no organization, I created presentation templates that uses our project team colors and style to better unify CUSD as a whole.










This semester, our team took individual headshots of every member on CUSD and asked them each to give us a quote describing their experience on CUSD thusfar. We then created customized thank-you cards for each member (left). We also created certificates for members who show the most contributions to reward and inspire them to keep up their amazing work. Every semester, we are constantly brainstorming new ideas on how to improve our exhibition for future semesters to come.
